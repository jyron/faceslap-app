FaceSlap — Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 17, 2026

FaceSlap is designed to respect your privacy completely.

What we collect

Nothing leaves your device. FaceSlap has no user accounts, no analytics, no advertising, and no third-party tracking of any kind. We — the developer — never receive, store, or have access to any of your data, including any face data.

Face data

Because face data is sensitive, here is exactly what FaceSlap does with it.

What face data is processed. When you pick a photo or take one with the camera, FaceSlap uses Apple's on-device Vision framework to analyze that single image. Specifically it computes:

  • a face bounding box (the rectangle where a face appears in the photo), and
  • facial landmark points — the approximate 2D positions of facial features such as the eyes, eyebrows, nose, mouth, cheeks, and jawline, and
  • a foreground subject mask used to cut the head out of the background.

These are simple geometric coordinates (for example, "the left cheek is at this x/y point in the image"). FaceSlap does not create a face template, faceprint, or any biometric signature, and it does not attempt to identify, recognize, verify, or match who the person is.

How it is used. The landmark points are used solely to place the cartoon gameplay overlays in the right spots — for example, putting a soft mark on a cheek, dizzy stars near the eyes, or spinning stars above the head — and to animate the springy wobble when you tap. That is the only purpose.

Where it is processed and how long it is kept. All of this happens entirely on your device, in memory, while you play. Examples:

  • The photo you choose and the face data derived from it are never uploaded to any server and are never transmitted off your device.
  • We retain nothing — there is no server, no account, and no database, so there is nowhere for your face data to be stored by us.
  • The processed image and landmarks live only in the app's temporary memory for your current session and are discarded when you pick a new face or close the app. They are not written to any permanent file unless you explicitly export a clip (see "Sharing").

Who it is shared with. No one. Your face data is not shared with the developer, with Apple, with advertisers, or with any third party.

Photos and camera

The camera and photo-library permissions are used only so you can choose an image to play with, as described in "Face data" above. Your photos and any videos you create never leave your device and are not accessible to the developer or any third party.

Sharing

If you export a clip and choose to share it, that uses Apple's standard share sheet. What you do with the file afterward is governed by those apps' and services' own privacy policies, not FaceSlap.

Children

FaceSlap does not knowingly collect any information from anyone, including children.
